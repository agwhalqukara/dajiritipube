Maurice DuBois Biography

Maurice DuBois is one of the most popular and richest Journalist who was born on August 20, 1965 in Long Island, New York, United States. Maurice DuBois, born August 20, 1965, is an American television anchorman who works for WCBS-TV New York City and CBS.

DuBois joined WCBS TV in September 2004 as an anchor for CBS 2 News at 6, which is the CBS East Coast flagship station. He has been co-anchoring CBS 2 News This Morning with Cindy Hsu, and CBS 2 News at Noon together with Mary Calvi.

DuBois is involved in various community organizations including serving on three non-profit boards – Pencil; Susan G. Komen for the Cure (New York City affiliate); and New York City Center. He has worked with WNET’s GED program.

In addition to covering local news, DuBois has worked as a reporter, covering national political conventions, AIDS in South Africa, witnessing a double execution – an experience which DuBois described as “intense”, the death of Pope John Paul II and the installation of Pope Benedict XVI.

DuBois was married to Andrea DuBois (nee Adair) on August 13, 2001. They have two sons, and they live together in Harlem.
