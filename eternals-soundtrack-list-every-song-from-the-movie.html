Eternals Soundtrack List: Every Song From the Movie

As with most other big-budget films, Marvel’s Eternals has a soundtrack featuring some high profile songs, alongside the film’s stunning score, which was composed by Ramin Djawadi (Game of Thrones, Westworld, Iron Man, Pacific Rim). While Disney has officially released the Djawadi-composed Original Motion Picture Soundtrack (you can listen below!), they have yet to give any information on the potential release of the accompanying bops featured in the film, including tracks from BTS and Lizzo.

The Original Motion Picture is rich and epic—what we’ve come to expect from Djawadi’s work—but let’s talk about the rest of the musical landscape for the MCU film. We’ll keep you updated on purchase information for the pop and rock-driven soundtrack if and when it becomes available. For now, check out the track list…

Eternals Full Movie Soundtrack List

Marvels’ Eternals has one of the more eclectic soundtracks in the MCU. Unlike Guardians of the Galaxy or even Captain Marvel, it is not channeling a specific era’s nostalgia so much as trying to bring together musical references from across the ages to accompany the film’s millennia-spanning story. While most of the film’s soundtrack features tracks from the 20th and 21st centuries, there are also some late Baroque bops. (Though, sadly, no Harry Styles.) Check out the full tracklist below:

“Time,” Pink Floyd
“Sugarfoot,” Black Joe Lewis and The Honeybears
“Stay In The Groove Pt. 1” The Dap-Kings (ft. Rickey Calloway)
“Losing You,” Written by Pierre Havet, Jean Gatson Renard, and Carl Sigman
“Nach Mera Hero,” Celina Sharma
“Friends,” BTS
“Lend Me Your Comb,” Carl Perkins
“Mama Tried,” Merle Haggard
“Juice,” Lizzo
“Happy Children,” Written by Harry Bluestone and Emil Cadkin
“Graceful Nature,” Written by Stuart Roslyn
“The End of the World,” Written by Stuart Roslyn
“Brandenburg Concerto No. 5 in D Major 2nd Mov. Affettuoso,” Composed by Bach
Prelude in G Major, BMV 902, No. 1A, Bach
“Feels Like the First Time,” Foreigner
