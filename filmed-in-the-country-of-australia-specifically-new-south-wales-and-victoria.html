Netflix’s Mind Your Manners; Filming Location: The Facelift Series Was Shot in Australia, Specifically in the States of New South Wales and Victoria; Some of the Show’s Scenes Were Located at la Porte Space!

Talking about its filming locations, Netflix's Mind Your Manners was shot in Australia, specifically in the states of New South Wales and Victoria. Principal photography for the first season of the reality series appears to have begun in November 2021 and ended in early January 2022. So, without further ado, let's take a look at all of the specific locations where the Netflix show is shot!

A significant portion of 'Mind Your Manners' is shot in New South Wales, Australia's most populous east coast state. Sydney, the state's capital, serves as one of the series' primary production locations, with most of the scenes shot at La Porte Space, a co-working space at 87/103 Epsom Road in Rosebery, and SKYE Suites at 300 Kent Street in Sydney.

For the first season's third episode, the production team used two more Sydney locations: Dress for Success in Marrickville at 132 Marrickville Road and Jin Wu Koon Muay Thai Kickboxing and Kung Fu Academy at Level 1/232-236 Cleveland Street in Chippendale. In addition, the archery scenes in episode 4 of the first season were shot on location at Sydney Archery on Hugh Bamford Reserve in North Bondi.

Furthermore, several scenes for the first season were shot at All Saint Clinic, 50 Bay Street, Double Bay, Joh Bailey Salon, 7 Knox Street, Double Bay, and Palace Chinese Restaurant, Shop 38 Level 1 Piccadilly Tower, 133/145 Castlereagh Street. Several films and television shows, including 'Thor: Love and Thunder,' 'Three Thousand Years of Longing,' 'Heartbreak High,' and 'Nine Perfect Strangers,' have been shot in Sydney.

The second-most populous state of Australia, Victoria, serves as the location for additional segments of "Mind Your Manners." Sara Jane Ho and the rest of the cast and crew travel across the state to film important scenes for the show. A few scenes for the first season were shot at Groomed to Go in Frankston and Caffe e Cucina at 581 Chapel Street in South Yarra.

Sara Jane Ho’s Career!

Sara Jane Ho (@sarajaneho) was born in China, but she studied at Harvard Business School and Georgetown University in the United States. She's a natural talent with degrees to back it up. Sara has a background in finance, despite being recognized as a manners expert for opening China's first finishing school in 2013.

According to LinkedIn, she worked for Perella Weinberg Partners and Morgan Stanley. In 2014, Fast Company named her etiquette school, Institute Sarita, one of the "World's Most Innovative 50 Companies." Sara now has two etiquette schools, the second of which will open in Shanghai in 2015. According to the BBC, studying at Institute Sarita is not cheap, with a 10-day debutante course costing around £8,500.

According to SCMP, Institute Sarita's 12-day hostess course costs slightly more than £12,000. She also has a YouTube channel where she teaches etiquette tips and has nearly 10,000 subscribers. Sara also authored a book, Finishing Touch: Good Manners for the Debutante, which was published in 2016.
